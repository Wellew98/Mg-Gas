Gas installation, COC & SANS 10087 FAQs

Do I need a Certificate of Compliance (COC) for my gas installation?

Yes. Under the Pressure Equipment Regulations of the Occupational Health and Safety Act, a valid Gas Certificate of Compliance is required after any new gas installation, alteration, or change of property ownership. It confirms the installation has been inspected and is safe and leak-free — and insurers can decline gas-related claims if a valid COC isn't in place.

What is SANS 10087?

SANS 10087 is the South African National Standard covering the safe handling, storage and installation of LP gas. Part 1 applies to typical home, restaurant and business cylinder installations, setting the requirements for pipework, ventilation, cylinder storage and equipment that a compliant installation must meet.

Is MG Gas Company's installation work SANS 10087 compliant?

Yes. Every installation we complete — from a single gas geyser to a full restaurant reticulation system — is done to SANS 10087 and issued with a Certificate of Compliance on completion.
